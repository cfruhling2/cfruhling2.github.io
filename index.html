COLTON FRUHLING

Colton Fruhling

I am a Postdoc at Purdue University working with Professors Shalaev, Boltasseva and Kildishev. My research interests broadly cover nonlinear optics and ultrafast light-matter interactions. My past research was focused on the nonlinear Thomson scattering where I studied coherency, nonlinearity and polarization aspects of the emitted light. More recently I have interesting in lasing on the nanoscale where I have studied random lasing in thin film perovskites. I am also avidly exploring the burgeoning field of time-varying media in the optical regime.

An Illustration of coherent scattering as a group of closely pack rocks are dropped into a pond

I graduated with a BS in Physics and Astronomy from Colorado State University where I worked in a spintronics lab directed by Dr. Kristen Buchanan. In 2021, completed my PhD at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln working in the Extreme Light Laboratory. I enjoy all outdoor activies and spending time with my family.
