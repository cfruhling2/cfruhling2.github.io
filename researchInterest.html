COLTON FRUHLING

Hi, thanks for stopping by

I am a graduate student at the University of Nebraksa-Lincoln in the Extreme Light Laboratory. My doctoral research has been primarily focused on nonlinear optical interactions with matter. Specifically I have studied nonlinear Thomson scattering where a ultra-high intensity laser induces relativistic motion in electrons. The relativistic motion gives rise to a nonlinearity causing harmonics to be radiated from the accelerated charge.

I am, however, interested in all nonlinear optical phenomenon. Some of which are common place in a high-powered laser system such as DIOCLES. I am also extremely interest in the developing field of quantum optics, especially with regard to optical computation and communication.
